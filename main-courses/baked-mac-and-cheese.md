# Adam's Baked Mac & Cheese

A 13×9 pan of creamy, cheesy, crispy-topped comfort.

## Info

- **Yield:** One 13×9 pan (8-10 servings)
- **Time:** ~45 min (20 prep, 25-30 bake)
- **Oven:** 350°F

## Ingredients

### Pasta
- 1 lb elbow macaroni

### Cheese Sauce
- 6 tbsp butter
- 6 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 4 cups whole milk
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp dry mustard
- 1/4 tsp cayenne (optional)
- 1 tsp hot sauce

### Cheese
- 8 oz sharp cheddar, shredded
- 8 oz Colby Jack, shredded
- 4 oz Monterey Jack, shredded
- Small handful extra cheddar or Colby Jack (for topping)

### Topping
- 1 cup Italian-seasoned panko
- 2 tbsp melted butter

## Instructions

1. Cook macaroni just shy of al dente; drain.
2. Melt butter in a pot. Whisk in flour; cook 1–2 minutes.
3. Slowly whisk in milk; simmer until thickened.
4. Add salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dry mustard, cayenne, and hot sauce.
5. Stir in all 20 oz cheese (reserve a small handful if desired).
6. Fold sauce into pasta. Pour into greased 13×9 dish.
7. Top with reserved cheese. Mix panko with melted butter and spread over top.
8. Bake at 350°F for 25–30 minutes until golden and bubbling.

## Notes

- **Thickness:** 6 tbsp flour to 4 cups milk (1.5 tbsp per cup) creates a sturdier béchamel than standard recipes. Combined with 20 oz of cheese and a 25-30 minute bake, this produces a rich, dense mac & cheese that holds its shape — not a thin, Alfredo-like sauce.
- **Cheese reduction:** Want it lighter? Drop the Monterey Jack (8 + 8 + 0 = 16 oz total). The sauce will still be thick but less overwhelming.
- **Egg option:** For even more set/custard texture, beat 1 egg into the milk before adding to the roux. Don't let it scramble — temper it with a splash of warm milk first.
- **Shred your own:** Pre-shredded cheese comes coated with cellulose and starch to prevent clumping — it also prevents smooth melting. Buy blocks and shred yourself for a creamier, more cohesive sauce.
