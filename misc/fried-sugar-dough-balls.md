# Fried Sugar Dough Balls

Soft, pillowy fried dough balls coated in sugar — a simple yeasted dough that's proofed, fried, and tossed in sugar while still hot.

## Info

- **Yield:** About 8 dough balls (tennis ball sized)
- **Time:** ~2.5 hours (including proofing)
- **Oil temp:** 350°F

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 1 cup warm milk
- 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1½ cups bread flour
- 4 tbsp butter, softened
- ¼ cup sugar
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt

### For Frying & Coating
- Oil for deep frying
- Granulated sugar for coating

### Optional
- Custard filling (any prepared pastry cream)

## Instructions

1. In a bowl, combine warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Whisk gently and let sit for about 10 minutes until frothy.
2. Add all-purpose flour, bread flour, butter, sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
3. Knead slowly until the dough comes together, then work it until smooth.
4. Form the dough into a smooth ball.
5. Cover with a damp cloth and let rest until doubled in size (about 1-2 hours).
6. While it rests, prepare a custard filling if desired.
7. After proofing, turn the dough out onto a floured work surface and divide into balls about the size of a tennis ball.
8. Cover and let proof again until nearly doubled (about 30-45 minutes).
9. Heat oil to 350°F.
10. Carefully place dough balls into the hot oil — fry until golden, flipping halfway through.
11. Remove from oil and immediately coat in granulated sugar while still hot.

## Notes

- The "80Y" mentioned in the original video refers to yeast — use one standard packet of active dry yeast.
- The dough should be smooth and elastic before the first proof. Don't rush it.
- Flipping halfway through ensures even cooking — the balls should be deep golden all around.
- Coating in sugar while hot is key — it sticks better and creates a light, sweet crust.
- For a filled version, pipe custard into the cooled balls using a piping tip.
- These are best eaten fresh and warm.
